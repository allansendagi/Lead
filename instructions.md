OVERVIEW
The existing site at ainavsystem.com is a single-page HTML quiz application. The rebuild transforms it into a multi-page content and lead generation platform with four distinct sections: the quiz (existing, preserved), a resources library (20 AEO pages, already written), an industries section (120 programmatic SEO pages, generated from template), and supporting pages (About, Work With Us).
The site has one commercial goal: generate qualified leads for the AI Navigator consulting engagement and direct them to WhatsApp or a discovery call booking. Every page design and structural decision is subordinate to this goal.

TECHNOLOGY STACK RECOMMENDATIONS
Option A — Recommended for speed and SEO:
Webflow (CMS) for the content pages + existing HTML quiz preserved as an embed or iframe on the home page. Webflow's CMS handles the 20 AEO pages easily. The 120 programmatic pages are generated using Webflow's CMS API or a CSV import, with the template applied automatically.
Option B — Recommended for developer control:
Next.js (React) with a headless CMS (Contentful or Sanity) for content management. The quiz stays as existing HTML, served as a component. The programmatic pages are generated using Next.js static site generation (SSG) with the industry × city dataset as the data source.
Option C — Simplest for a solo developer:
WordPress with a lightweight theme + Yoast SEO plugin for schema markup + ACF (Advanced Custom Fields) for the programmatic page template. The quiz is embedded via iframe.
Regardless of option chosen, the following requirements are non-negotiable and apply to all three options.

NON-NEGOTIABLE TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
1. Page speed — Core Web Vitals
Every page must score 90+ on Google PageSpeed Insights (mobile and desktop). This is a hard requirement for SEO competitiveness. No heavy page builders, no unoptimised images, no render-blocking scripts.
2. Schema markup — FAQ schema on every page
Every page that contains a FAQ section must have FAQ schema markup applied correctly. This is the primary mechanism for AEO — appearing as cited answers in ChatGPT and Perplexity. Use JSON-LD format, placed in the <head> of each page. Test every page with Google's Rich Results Test before launch.
3. Schema markup — Author schema on Page 4
Page 4 (/resources/ai-navigator-framework) must have Article schema with Author entity pointing to Allan Sendagi, including sameAs links to his LinkedIn profile and any other authoritative profiles. This establishes him as the creator of the framework in Google's Knowledge Graph.
4. Mobile-first design
Qatar's internet usage is predominantly mobile. Every page must be fully functional and visually correct on a 390px viewport (iPhone 14 size) before desktop is considered. The quiz especially — test on actual mobile devices, not just browser simulation.
5. Arabic language preparation
The site launches in English. Arabic pages are coming in Phase 2. The developer must build the URL and CMS structure to support Arabic without a full rebuild — using /ar/ subdirectory structure (ainavsystem.com/ar/resources/[slug]/) and hreflang tags prepared for Phase 2 addition. Do not launch Arabic pages — just prepare the structure.
6. WhatsApp CTA button — persistent across all pages
A floating WhatsApp button must appear on every page, bottom-right corner, linking to the business WhatsApp number. This is the primary conversion mechanism. The button must be visible on mobile without obscuring content. Use the standard WhatsApp green (#25D366). Link format: https://wa.me/[WHATSAPP_NUMBER]?text=Hi%20Allan%2C%20I%27m%20interested%20in%20the%20AI%20Navigator%20framework
7. Internal linking — every page links to the quiz
Every resource page and industry page must contain at minimum two links to the quiz (ainavsystem.com home page) — one mid-page CTA and one in the author bio at the bottom. The quiz is the primary lead capture mechanism.
8. Sitemap and Google Search Console
A complete XML sitemap must be generated and submitted to Google Search Console on launch day. The sitemap must include all 20 AEO pages and all 120 programmatic pages. Update the sitemap automatically when new pages are published.
9. Robots.txt
Standard robots.txt allowing all crawlers. No noindex tags on any content pages. The only pages that should be noindexed are any admin, staging, or test pages.
10. SSL and HTTPS
All pages must serve over HTTPS. Enforce HTTPS redirect from HTTP. This is a baseline requirement, not an optional feature.

You are transforming ainavsystem.com from a single-page quiz tool into the dominant AI consulting content destination for Qatar and the GCC.
The quiz stays exactly as it is — it works, it captures leads, it drives WhatsApp conversations. Everything being added sits alongside it, not instead of it.
The content library you have just built — 20 deep AEO pages totalling approximately 47,000 words — becomes the trust-building layer between your ads and your close. Right now, the journey is: ad → WhatsApp → close. The content layer adds a step: ad → research → content → WhatsApp → close. That research step is where most high-value clients spend time before committing. You want to own everything they read during that research.
The 120 programmatic pages are the volume play. They capture every permutation of "AI for [industry] in [city]" that anyone in the GCC might search — and they do it at a scale that would take years to produce manually.

Your Three Content Priorities in Order
Priority 1 — Get the 20 AEO pages live first. These are already written. The developer uploads them, adds schema markup, and publishes. This is the highest-leverage action because these pages target the highest-intent queries — people who are already close to making a decision. Every week these pages are not live is a week of compounding SEO opportunity you are not capturing.
Priority 2 — Generate and publish the 120 programmatic pages. These require the developer to build the generation system, run the AI content generation for each page, review a sample, and publish in batches. Publish the Qatar pages first — all 15 industries in Doha — then expand to Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, and the other cities. Qatar is your home market and ranks fastest because the competition is lowest.
Priority 3 — The supporting pages. About, Work With Me, and the Resources index page. These matter for conversion and navigation but are lower priority than the content that drives inbound traffic.

The Three Numbers to Watch After Launch
Once the site is live, three numbers tell you whether the strategy is working.
Google Search Console impressions. This shows how many times your pages appeared in Google search results, even if no one clicked. Impressions grow before clicks do — it is the leading indicator that Google is indexing your content and beginning to rank it. You should see impressions growing week-on-week from week 3 onwards. If impressions are flat after 6 weeks, there is an indexation or schema problem to diagnose.
Quiz completion rate. Of everyone who arrives at the quiz, what percentage completes it and submits their contact details? The current rate from your ads is your baseline. If the content pages are working — bringing in warm, high-intent visitors — the quiz completion rate from organic traffic should be higher than from cold ad traffic. If it is lower, there is a mismatch between what the content promises and what the quiz delivers.
WhatsApp button clicks from organic traffic. GA4 tracks this as a custom event. This is the direct revenue signal — people who arrived through search and then initiated a WhatsApp conversation. This number starts at zero and grows as organic traffic builds. By month 3, you should see the first organic-originated WhatsApp conversations. By month 6, organic should be contributing meaningfully to the overall lead volume alongside your ads.
